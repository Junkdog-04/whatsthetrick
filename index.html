WHAT'S THE TRICK?

LET'S STARTED...

As I listen to the song Wonderfall by Oasis (I really recommend listening to it when you're having a bad day or want to reflect, as in this case). At the same time, I realize that I want to help anyone who has doubts about programming or even about their career (not because there's something wrong with me, and I don't think I have savior syndrome... I hope), but rather I realize that my specialty is effectively teaching what your teachers are supposed to teach you in the classroom so that you can apply it. I like that, and it makes me happy to be aware of my catharsis, where I can show you that, as a human being, I have had successes and ups and downs in learning to be a better version of myself. So I want to ask you: Have you felt bad about the overwhelming advance of AI in your learning process to learn how to code?

I know you've heard that AI is going to take away coders' jobs, but that's not the case. Look, there's no overall objective for this tool as such, so much so that many companies are losing the money they've invested in research to exploit this tool to the fullest. Keep in mind that it's artificial intelligence because instead of providing solutions, it creates more problems (if you don't believe me, check out the tech news on this topic). If your parents or friends say to you: “Why are you studying software engineering if AI is going to do all the coding for existing systems?”, “Did you know that AI is going to do your job?” and my favorite so far, “And when you upload that system to GITHUB or make that video, are they going to pay you for it?” THE TRICK is to understand that AI doesn't know everything and that it's difficult for it to keep up with trends, just like you and me. They even tell you that it can make mistakes. As for the passive-aggressive well-wishes in the form of direct questions from your loved ones, don't react(Damn, I'm not talking about the Meta library). Sadly, they think that success is measured by money or the things you can have.

You're not the only one who suffers from this (that includes me, welcome to the club). THE TRICK is to understand that not everyone can code, that logic governs everything, and that when you understand how to translate it into code after many sleepless hours, you can finally move on to using AI because now you know how, but the BIGGEST TRICK IS... 


THE TRICK isn't in the lines of code you have, nor is it in how much memory you can use on the server when running a
process, and you're certainly not going to find the trick by reading thousands of books or learning a new trendy
framework as quickly as possible.

THE DAMN TRICK is to understand once and for all that your mind is wonderful for being slow and that this is healthy,
and that there is no point in accumulating knowledge if you do nothing with it, because that is what sickens your soul
and existence.

If you understand this, the rest is a piece of cake. And please don't be lazy and install so many npm dependencies in
your projects to “save development time.” Instead, see what that dependency does, understand its benefits, and try to
replicate it, and you'll see that you have more magic than you thought you did.
